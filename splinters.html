You love me, but

Rusty keys, defective lock

Infiltrations, humidity in the dungeons

It penetrates the bones, and they will creak, crack, rot, give way.

You can’t enter; look through the bars

Inside, you can’t get out; look through the bars

You reach out a hand, in vain

Locked, broken lock, you can’t pass through

You can’t be freed

Dented, twisted keys, there’s no way, even if you wanted to.

Decomposed skeleton, piled up,

Little blood spilled, lumps of chewed-up flesh, a heart, splinters.

There is no abandonment, forgetfulness, it happens.
